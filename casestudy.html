Redesign of a non-profit website

Catering to the needs of the stakeholder

Overview

Working in a team to redesign an existing nonprofit organization website. Using data from user-centered research and insight from the stakeholder to create the best possible result.

Problem

The nonproft, French Bulldog Rescue Network, was desperate for a redesign.

  • CTA buttons were difficult to find.
  • Navigation and texts on the site failed color accessibility test.
  • Long page of texts with a lack of hierarchy.
  • An outdated design.

Solution

Redesign the website that focuses on the stakeholder's main concerns, as well as making the content on the site legible, to make it a user-friendly experience.

Role

UXUI Designer

  • Research
  • Prototype
  • Testing
  • Iteration
  • Finalize

Research

Redline Annotation Competitor Analysis User Interviews & Surveys User Persona User Journey Map User Flow

The team wanted to understand the formatting of the current website and discover what was important enough to be displayed. This task was given to a team member.

According to his annotation:

  • The site was considerate of Covid-19 and had a banner on the very top. There were duplicates.
  • Navigation bar failed the Color Accessibility. In terms of responsive web design, the navigation bar would stack.
  • The placement of images were out of place. Text would wrap around the images.
  • CTA buttons were hard to detect.

On the websites, users could do four things:

  • Donate
  • Adopt
  • Volunteer
  • Shop

After annotating the current FBRN website, each team member picked a similar site to do a SWOT competitor analysis.

I chose Animal Humane Society because it focused on the same purpose as FBRN: donation, volunteering, adopting. They also provided other services that provided FBRN opportunities.

Major things I noticed about AHS:

  • Their Donate button is everywhere and is a noticeable color. FBRN only displayed it once and was very hard to find.
  • I also took note of how their navigation bar was set up (their sitemap).
  • They had their 3 priorities, right on the home page: Adopt, Donate, and Volunteer.


For our user interviews, we decided to focus on current pet owners (bought or adopt). While writing out the user research plan, we chose to focus on these three objectives:

  • Recognizing the user's preference in pet features
  • Understanding what the user's thought process were when adopting a pet
  • Knowing the requisites for donating to certain non-profits
  • Asking the user's opinion and observing their reaction on the current FBRN website

During the interview phase, we decided to get some quantitative data on current pet owners. Through the surveys, we focused on:

  • Knowing the most important pet features
  • How many users would consider adoption
